OBSERVATIONS 25 DCMR CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
1. - Neither the Certified Food Protection Manager certificate issued by the DC Department of Health nor the establishment's business license are conspicuously posted in the restaurant. (Corrected On Site)  200.5 Documentation that at least one (1) full-time equivalent person in charge is a certified food protection manager or a certified limited food protection manager with demonstrated knowledge of food safety, as specified in sections 200.3 and 200.4, shall be conspicuously posted in the food establishment next to the food establishment license. Such documentation shall be removed when the individual is no longer employed on-site by the establishment.  
2. - The establishment does not have an employee health policy for foodborne illness. No signed employee interview and reporting forms exist regarding the prevention of the transmission of diseases through food by infected food employees. I provided the establishment with a copy of the DC Food Code Medical referral, employee interview, and reporting agreement forms. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  300.1 The licensee shall require food employees and food employee applicants to whom a conditional offer of employment is made to report to the person in charge information about their health and activities as they relate to diseases that are transmissible through food.  
3. - No employee health policy for foodborne illness. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  302.2 The person in charge may remove a restriction specified in section 301.2(a) if the restricted person: (a) Is free of the symptoms as specified in section 300.4 and no foodborne illness occurs that may have been caused by the restricted person; (b) Is suspected of causing foodborne illness but is free of the symptoms specified in section 300.4, and provides written medical documentation from a physician licensed to practice medicine stating that the restricted person is free of the infectious agent that is suspected of causing the person?s symptoms or causing foodborne illness, as specified in Chapter 45, section 4503; or (c) Provides written medical documentation from a physician licensed to practice medicine stating that the symptoms experienced result from a chronic noninfectious condition such as Crohn's disease, irritable bowel syndrome, or ulcerative colitis.  
8. - No handwashing signage at the bar handwashing sink. (Corrected On Site)  3004.1 A sign or poster that notifies food employees to wash their hands shall be provided at all handwashing lavatories used by food employees and shall be clearly visible to food employees.  
8. - A plastic container holding numerous cups is stored in the room service handwashing sink. (Corrected On Site)  2414.2 A handwashing lavatory may not be used for purposes other than handwashing.  
13. - The raw chicken in the walk-in refrigerator is stored above the tuna. (Corrected On Site)  802.1 Food shall be protected from cross contamination by: (b) Except when combined as ingredients, separating types of raw animal foods from each other such as beef, fish, lamb, pork, and poultry during storage, preparation, holding, and display by: (1) Using separate equipment for each type; or (2) Arranging each type of food in equipment so that cross contamination of one type with another is prevented, and preparing each type of food at different times or in separate areas.  
14. - There is mold on the ice machines' plastic drip panel and ice chute. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  1900.1 Equipment food-contact surfaces, and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch.  
14. - The dishwash machine's final rinse temperature was measured at 150 degrees Fahrenheit which is below the data plate requirement therefore utensils are not being sanitized during the fresh hot water final rinse cycle. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  2002.1 After being cleaned, equipment, food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be sanitized in: (b) Hot water mechanical operations by being cycled through equipment that is set up as specified in sections 1804, 1811, and 1812 and achieving a utensil surface temperature of 71 degrees C (160 degrees F) as measured by an irreversible registering temperature indicator.  
14. - The quaternary ammonium sanitizing solutions are held at temperatures below 75 degrees Fahrenheit. (Corrected On Site)  1813.4 A quaternary ammonium compound solution shall: (a) Have a minimum temperature of 24 degrees C (75 degrees F); (b) Have a concentration as specified in section 3404 and as indicated by the manufacturer's use directions included in the labeling; and (c) Be used only in water with 500 mg/L hardness or less, or in water having a hardness no greater than specified by the manufacturer's label.  
34. - There are no visible thermometers in the room service under counter refrigerator and the restaurant beverage refrigerator. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1524.2 Cold or hot holding equipment used for potentially hazardous food shall be designed to include and shall be equipped with at least one integral or permanently affixed temperature measuring device that is located to allow easy viewing of the device's temperature display, except as specified in section 1524.3.  
34. - The final rinse temperature gauge on the main kitchen dishwash machine is out of order. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1816.3 Ambient air temperature, water pressure, and water temperature measuring devices shall be maintained in good repair and be accurate within the intended range of use.  
35. - There are numerous unlabeled plastic squeeze bottles of various liquids and unlabeled containers of nuts and seeds on the kitchen shelves. (Corrected On Site)  803.1 Working containers holding food or food ingredients that are removed from their original packages for use in the food establishment, such as cooking oils, flour, herbs, potato flakes, salt, spices, and sugar shall be identified with the common name of the food except that containers holding food that can be readily and unmistakably recognized such as dry pasta need not be identified.  
38. - Cooks and other employees preparing food in the kitchen are wearing watches and braceletes on their wrists. (Corrected On Site)  407.1 While preparing food, food employees may not wear jewelry on their arms and hands. This section does not apply to a plain ring such as a wedding band.  
42. - The utensils on the drying racks in the dishwash area along with the plates and bowls on the shelf above the steam table and salad bar in the employee cafeteria have their food-contact surfaces exposed and they need to be inverted. (Corrected On Site)  2203.2 Clean equipment and utensils shall be stored as specified in section 2203.1 and shall be stored: (b) Covered or inverted.  
45. - The cutting boards have numerous deep grooves and are chipped. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  1502.1 Multiuse food-contact surfaces shall be: (a) Smooth; (b) Free of breaks, open seams, cracks, chips, pits, inclusions, and similar imperfections;  
46. - The dishwash machine in the banquet kitchen does not have a pressure gauge installed. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITH 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1530.1 Warewashing machines that provide a fresh hot water sanitizing rinse shall be equipped with a pressure gauge or similar device such as a transducer that measures and displays the water pressure in the supply line immediately before entering the warewashing machine.  
46. - The main kitchen's dishwash machine data plate does not list the pressure required for sanitizing. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITH 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1525.1 A warewashing machine shall be provided with an easily accessible and readable data plate affixed to the machine by the manufacturer that indicates the machine's design and operating specifications including the: (b) Pressure required for the fresh water sanitizing rinse unless the machine is designed to use only a pumped sanitizing rinse.  
47. - The interior of the main kitchen's dishwash machine needs to be delimed. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1903.1 Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be cleaned at a frequency necessary to preclude accumulation of soil residues  
54. - Blown bulb (no lighting) in the display refrigerator in the kitchen. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3008.1(b) The light intensity shall be: (b) At least two hundred and twenty (220) lux or twenty (20) foot candles: (2) Inside equipment such as reach-in and under-counter refrigerators.  
54. - The ceiling light tubes (4) above the 3-compartment sink are exposed. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  2908.1 Except as specified in section 2908.2, light bulbs shall be shielded, coated, or otherwise shatter-resistant in areas where there is exposed food, clean equipment, utensils, linens, or unwrapped single-service and single-use articles.
