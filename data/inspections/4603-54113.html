OBSERVATIONS 25 DCMR CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
1. - Neither the business license nor the certified food protection manager information are posted in a conspicuous location for the public to view. (Corrected On Site)  200.5 Documentation that at least one (1) full-time equivalent person in charge is a certified food protection manager or a certified limited food protection manager with demonstrated knowledge of food safety, as specified in sections 200.3 and 200.4, shall be conspicuously posted in the food establishment next to the food establishment license. Such documentation shall be removed when the individual is no longer employed on-site by the establishment.  
2. - No written employee health policy for foodborne illness provided. The orientation package for employees does not address foodborne illness and the computer test required for employees could not be verified.  300.1 The licensee shall require food employees and food employee applicants to whom a conditional offer of employment is made to report to the person in charge information about their health and activities as they relate to diseases that are transmissible through food.  
3. - I was unable to verify an employee health policy for foodborne illnesses.  302.2 The person in charge may remove a restriction specified in section 301.2(a) if the restricted person: (a) Is free of the symptoms as specified in section 300.4 and no foodborne illness occurs that may have been caused by the restricted person; (b) Is suspected of causing foodborne illness but is free of the symptoms specified in section 300.4, and provides written medical documentation from a physician licensed to practice medicine stating that the restricted person is free of the infectious agent that is suspected of causing the person?s symptoms or causing foodborne illness, as specified in Chapter 45, section 4503; or (c) Provides written medical documentation from a physician licensed to practice medicine stating that the symptoms experienced result from a chronic noninfectious condition such as Crohn's disease, irritable bowel syndrome, or ulcerative colitis.  
6. - An employee washed her hands for approximately 8 seeconds after she was observed wiping her nose on her gloves and she did not wash her exposed arms. The establishment's handwashing policy needs to be reinforced by management.  401.1 Except as specified in section 401.2, food employees shall clean their hands and exposed portions of their arms with cleaning compound in a lavatory that is equipped as specified in section 2402 by vigorously rubbing together the surfaces of their lathered hands and arms for at least twenty (20) seconds and thoroughly rinsing with clean water. Employees shall pay particular attention to the areas underneath the fingernails and between the fingers.  
8. - Pans and other food prep items were in the handwashing sink in the vegetable prep section in the basement. (Corrected On Site)  2414.2 A handwashing lavatory may not be used for purposes other than handwashing.  
8. - Three (3) of the handwashing sinks do not have any handwashing signage posted. (Corrected On Site)  3004.1 A sign or poster that notifies food employees to wash their hands shall be provided at all handwashing lavatories used by food employees and shall be clearly visible to food employees.  
14. - Sheets of ice are on the floor and the wooden pallets in the walk-in freezer. Encrusted food is left on some of the baking pans that are drying on the racks in the basement.  1900.1 Equipment food-contact surfaces, and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch.  
14. - The temperature sensitive strips did not register at least 160 degrees Fahrenheit when run through 2 complete dishwasher cycles.  2002.1 After being cleaned, equipment, food-contact surfaces and utensils shall be sanitized in: (b) Hot water mechanical operations by being cycled through equipment that is set up as specified in sections 1804, 1811, and 1812 and achieving a utensil surface temperature of 71 degrees C (160 degrees F) as measured by an irreversible registering temperature indicator.  
34. - There are no visible functioning thermometers in certain refrigeration units.  1524.2 Cold or hot holding equipment used for potentially hazardous food shall be designed to include and shall be equipped with at least one integral or permanently affixed temperature measuring device that is located to allow easy viewing of the device's temperature display, except as specified in section 1524.3.  
34. - The dishwasher's thermometer and the walk-in freezer thermometer are out of order. There is no visible readout on the digital thermometer in the freezer with the frozen hamburgers and no internal thermometer was supplied.  1816.3 Ambient air temperature, water pressure, and water temperature measuring devices shall be maintained in good repair and be accurate within the intended range of use.  
36. - Rodent droppings were observed in the stoarge room under the pallets. (Corrected On Site)  3210.1(b) The presence of insects, rodents, and other pests shall be controlled to minimize their presence on the premises by: (b) Routinely inspecting the premises for evidence of pests.  
36. - There are gaps in the ceiling tiles around the hoses leading from the syrup tank to the ceiling in the soda room.  2912.1 Except as otherwise specified in this section, the outer openings of a food establishment shall be protected against the entry of insects and rodents by: (a) Filling or closing holes and other gaps along floors, walls, and ceilings; (b) Closed, tight-fitting windows; and (c) Solid, self-closing, tight-fitting doors.  
38. - Two (2) of the employees who are preparing sandwiches and the employee scooping the fries are not wearing hair restraints. The person in charge stated that these employees are managers and McDonald's policy is that management does not have to wear hair restraints.  502.1 Food employees shall wear hair restraints such as hats, hair coverings or nets, beard restraints, and clothing that covers body hair, that are designed and worn to effectively keep their hair from contacting exposed food; clean equipment, utensils, and linens; and unwrapped single-service and single-use articles.  
39. - Wiping cloths are stored on the cutting boards and no sanitizing solutions were prepared or visible.  812.2 Cloths used for wiping food spills shall be: (b) Wet and cleaned as specified in section 2101.4, stored in a chemical sanitizer at a concentration specified in section 1813, and used for wiping spills from food-contact and nonfood contact surfaces of equipment.  
42. - Sleeves of cups are stored under the counter less than 6 inches above the floor in the front of the store.  2203.1 Cleaned equipment and utensils, laundered linens, and single-service and single-use articles, except as specified in section 2203.4, shall be stored: (c) At least fifteen centimeters (15 cm) or six inches (6 in.) above the floor.  
45. - The cutting boards are not smooth and some are badly chipped while others have noticeable grooves in them.  1502.1 Multiuse food-contact surfaces shall be: (a) Smooth; (b) Free of breaks, open seams, cracks, chips, pits, inclusions, and similar imperfections;  
46. - The final rinse temperature on the dishwasher could not be determined because the unit's thermometer is out of order. The temperature sensitive strips did not register at least 160 degrees Fahrenheit when run through 2 complete cycles.  1811.1 Except as specified in section 1811.2, in a mechanical operation, the temperature of the fresh hot water sanitizing rinse as it enters the manifold shall not be more than 90 degrees C (194 degrees F), or less than: (b) For all other machines, 82 degrees C (180 degrees F).  
46. - The dishwasher's wash temperature reached 108 degrees Fahrenheit. The data plate requirement is at least 150 degrees Fahrenheit.  1809.1 A warewashing machine and its auxiliary components shall be operated in accordance with the machine's data plate and other manufacturer's instructions.The temperature of the wash solution in spray type warewashers that use hot water to sanitize shall not be less than: (d) For a multi-tank, conveyor, multi-temperature machine, 66C (150F).  
46. - Dishwasher temperatures are not in compliance.  1804.1 A warewashing machine and its auxiliary components shall be operated in accordance with the machine's data plate and other manufacturer's instructions.  
47. - The curtains inside the dishwasher have an accumulation of food debris.  1900.3 Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris.  
48. - No hot water at the handwashing sinks upstairs and in the staff toilet rooms. Only cold water came out of the handwashing sinks upstairs and at the smoothie station - no water came out of 2 of the sinks when only the hot water knob was turned on.  2305.2 Hot water generation and distribution systems shall be sufficient to meet the peak hot water demands throughout the food establishment.  
49. - The garbage disposal unit in the dishwashing area is out of order. One handwashing sink is clogged and another handwashing sink has a leak. The drain (sewage) next to the washing machine is clogged (the drain was unclogged prior to completion of the inspection).  2418.1 A plumbing system shall be: (a) Repaired according to the 1996 BOCA Codes and 1999 D.C. Supplements; and (b) Maintained in good repair.  
53. - Wooden pallets are on the floor of the walk-in freezer.  2900.1 Except as specified in section 2903, the floors, floor coverings, walls, wall coverings, and ceilings shall be designed, constructed, and installed so they are smooth and easily cleanable, except that antislip floor coverings or applications may be used for safety reasons.  
53. - Rodent droppings observed in the storage room. (Corrected On Site)  3201.1 The physical facilities shall be cleaned as often as necessary to keep them clean.  
53. - The old equipment that is stored in the storage room needs to be discarded.  3213.1 The premises shall be free of: (a) Items that are unnecessary to the operation or maintenance of the establishment such as equipment that is nonfunctional or no longer used; and (b) Litter.  
54. - Blown bulb (inadequate lighting) in two (2) reach-in freezers upstairs.  3008.1(b) The light intensity shall be: (b) At least two hundred and twenty (220) lux or twenty (20) foot candles: (2) Inside equipment such as reach-in and under-counter refrigerators.
