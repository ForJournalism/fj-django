Six (6) or more CRITICAL VIOLATIONS that cannot be corrected on site during the course of the inspection results in an automatic suspension and closure of a food establishment.
OBSERVATIONS 25 DCMR CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
2. - The establishment does not have an employee health policy or can not provide proof of policy. No signed employee interview and reporting forms exist regarding the prevention of the transmission of diseases through food by infected food employees. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS) Employee Health Policy Forms discussed and issued during the inspection.  300.1 The licensee shall require food employees and food employee applicants to whom a conditional offer of employment is made to report to the person in charge information about their health and activities as they relate to diseases that are transmissible through food.  
6. - Observed improper/lack of proper handwashing by food preparation employees throughout. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  403.1 Food employees shall clean their hands and exposed portions of their arms as specified in section 401 immediately before engaging in food preparation including working with exposed food, clean equipment and utensils, and unwrapped single-service and single-use articles and: (a) After touching bare human body parts other than clean hands and clean, exposed portions of arms; (b) After using the toilet room; (c) After caring for or handling service or aquatic animals as specified in section 503.2; (d) Except as specified in section 500.2, after coughing, sneezing, using a handkerchief or disposable tissue, using tobacco, eating, or drinking; (e) After handling soiled equipment or utensils; (f) Immediately before engaging in food preparation including working with exposed food, clean utensils, and unwrapped single-service and single-use articles in the food preparation area; (g) During food preparation, as often as necessary to remove soil and contamination and to prevent cross-contamination when changing tasks; (h) When switching between working with raw food and working with ready-to-eat food; and (i) After engaging in other activities that contaminate the hands such as handling waste containing fecal matter, body fluids, or body discharges.  
6. - Observed food preparation employees wearing watches and jewelry during food preparation. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  407.2 While preparing food, food employees may not wear jewelry on their arms and hands. This section does not apply to a plain ring such as a wedding band.  
6. - Observed food preparation employees conducting handwashing in the 2-compartment sink. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  404.1 Food employees shall clean their hands in a handwashing lavatory or approved automatic handwashing facility and shall not clean their hands in a sink used for food preparation, or in a service sink or a curbed cleaning facility used for the disposal of mop water and similar liquid waste.  
6. - Observed employees preparing food located in the prep kitchen and on the waiters service line without wearing proper hair restraints. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  502.1 Food employees shall wear hair restraints such as hats, hair coverings or nets, beard restraints, and clothing that covers body hair, that are designed and worn to effectively keep their hair from contacting exposed food; clean equipment, utensils, and linens; and unwrapped single-service and single-use articles.  
7. - Observed food preparation employee using bare hand contact to handle flour taco shells on the stove top grill. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  800.2 Except when washing fruits and vegetables as specified in section 806 or when otherwise approved, food employees may not contact exposed, ready to eat food with their bare hands and shall use suitable utensils such as deli tissue, spatulas, tongs, single-use gloves or dispensing equipment.  
8. - Observed handwashing sinks located throughout the establishment in poor repair such as; the kitchen service cook line handwashing sink missing the cold water faucet, kitchen food preparation sink excessive leakage present(loose elbow pipe underneath unit), rear service bar handwashing sink slow water drainage. Observed unclean handwashing sinks throughout establishment. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3207.1 Handwashing lavatories shall be kept clean and maintained, and used as specified in section 2414  
14. - Observe unclean food-contact surfaces throughout such as; knife racks and knives, food prep tables and shelving throughout, dessert station ice machine interior, food mixers, food bins, dunnage racks, warewash dish racks, waiters room espresso wands and water machine spouts, soda hoses, main bar and rear bar soda gun holsters, rear frozen magarita machine dasher tray, refrigeration unit food shelving units throughout. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  1900.1 Equipment food-contact surfaces, and utensils shall be clean to sight and touch.  
16. - Observed chicken cooked on the grill and transfered to the oven to continue the cooking process, chicken cooked to an improper food temperature of 138F, chicken returned to the oven for a final cooked temperature of 165F. (Corrected On Site)  901.1 Raw animal foods cooked in a microwave oven shall be: (a) Rotated during cooking, stirred throughout during cooking, or stirred midway during cooking to compensate for uneven distribution of heat; (b) Covered to retain surface moisture; (c) Heated to a temperature of at least 74 degrees C (165 degrees F) in all parts of the food; and (d) Allowed to stand covered for two (2) minutes after cooking to obtain temperature equilibrium.  
20. - Observed cold food item(s) held at improper temperature(s) located on the cook line refrigerated service line such as; guacamole-49F. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  1005.1 Except during preparation, cooking, or cooling, or when time is used as the public health control as specified in section 1009, potentially hazardous food shall be maintained: (b) At 5 degrees C (41 degrees F) or less, except as specified in sections 1005.1(c), 1007, 1008 and 1523.  
31. - Observed tray of chicken flantas located in the walk-in refrigeration unit in the cooling process tightly coverd as not to allow the transfer of heat. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  1004.2 When placed in cooling or cold holding equipment, food containers in which food is being cooled shall be: (a) Arranged in the equipment to provide maximum heat transfer through the container walls; and (b) Loosely covered, or uncovered if protected from overhead contamination as specified in section 816.1(b), during the cooling period to facilitate heat transfer from the surface of the food.  
34. - Thermometers not located in reach-in refrigerators, located in the kitchen. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1524.1 In a mechanically refrigerated or hot food storage unit, the sensor of a temperature measuring device shall be located to measure the air temperature in the warmest part of a mechanically refrigerated unit and in the coolest part of a hot food storage unit.  
36. - Observed drain/fruit flies activity throughout the establishment. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  3210.1 (c ) The presence of insects, rodents, and other pests shall be controlled to minimize their presence on the premises by: (c) Using methods, if pests are found, such as trapping devices or other means of pest control as specified in sections 3402, 3410 and 3411.  
37. - Observed food cook line employee with tongs used to handle food stored on apron. Observed unclean towels covering food containers located in the food prep kitchen area. Uncovered food bottles located in the bar reach-in refrigeration units. Corrective action conducted during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  824.1 Food shall be protected from contamination that may result from a factor or source not specified in sections 800 - 823.  
41. - Observed in-use utensils, located at the grill station on the cook/service line in a container of water temperature 112F at call, container relocated on the grill to obtain a temperature of 140F or above during service/use. (Corrected On Site)  810.1  
45. - Cutting boards located throughout,in poor repair replaced during the inspection. (Corrected On Site)  1502.1 Multiuse food-contact surfaces shall be: (a) Smooth; (b) Free of breaks, open seams, cracks, chips, pits, inclusions, and similar imperfections;  
46. - Observed the warewashing machine sanitizing rinse cycle temperature below 180F, 150F at call, unit repaired during the inspection final rinse temperature 200F(temperature adjusted to 194F). (Corrected On Site)  1811.1 Except as specified in section 1811.2, in a mechanical operation, the temperature of the fresh hot water sanitizing rinse as it enters the manifold shall not be more than 90 degrees C (194 degrees F), or less than: (a) For a stationary rack, single temperature machine, 74 degrees C (165 degrees F); or (b) For all other machines, 82 degrees C (180 degrees F).  
47. - Observed unclean non-food contact exterior surfaces throughout the kitchen area. Observed unclean equipment transportation carts, observed unclean food equipment storage area, and unclean storage shelving units throughout, observed unclean rear bar lower shelving units. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  1900.3 Nonfood-contact surfaces of equipment shall be kept free of an accumulation of dust, dirt, food residue, and other debris.N  
49. - Observed mopsink faucet in poor repair not operational. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 5 CALENDAR DAYS)  2418.1 A plumbing system shall be: (a) Repaired according to the 1996 BOCA Codes and 1999 D.C. Supplements; and (b) Maintained in good repair.  
51. - Covered receptacles not provided in female toilet rooms. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  2707.1 A toilet room used by females shall be provided with a covered receptacle for sanitary napkins  
53. - Observed the following; gap in the warewashing station stainless steel wall causing water leakage on the floor(standing water), as well as mold issues in the chemical storage area and warewashing areas. Observed missing filter located on the warewashing hood system. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3200.1 The physical facilities shall be maintained in good repair.  
53. - Observed unclean floors behind and underneath equipment throughout establishment. Observed unclean lightshield covers and ceiling vents in the kitchen area. Observed food spillage present on the kitchen walls throughout. Observed missing ceiling tiles above the kitchen ice machine and warewashing area, observed mold present on walls located in the warewashing and chemical storage areas. Observed unclean grill on the cook line. Observed unclean floor drains throughout. Observed unclean walk-in fanguard covers. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3201.1 The physical facilities shall be cleaned as often as necessary to keep them clean.  
54. - Observed grease build-up present on the grill hood filters. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3203.1 Intake and exhaust air ducts shall be cleaned and filters changed so they are not a source of contamination by dust, dirt, and other materials.  
54. - Observed emloyees clothing and possessions stored on the dry fodd storage area shelving units and not in the provided lockers. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3010.2 Lockers or other suitable facilities shall be provided for the orderly storage of employees' clothing and other possessionsLockers or other suitable facilities shall be provided for the orderly storage of employees' clothing and other possessions  
54. - Observed inadequate lighting provided in the warewashing station. (CORRECT VIOLATION WITHIN 45 CALENDAR DAYS)  3008.1(b) Except as specified in section 2908.2, light bulbs shall be shielded, coated, or otherwise shatter-resistant in areas where there is exposed food, clean equipment, utensils, linens, or unwrapped single-service and single-use articles. The light intensity shall be: (b) At least two hundred and twenty (220) lux or twenty (20) foot candles: (1) At a surface where food is provided for consumer self-service, such as buffets and salad bars, or where fresh produce or packaged foods are sold or offered for consumption; (2) Inside equipment such as reach-in and under-counter refrigerators; (3) At a distance of seventy-five centimeters (75 cm) or thirty inches (30 in.) above the floor in areas used for handwashing, warewashing behind a bar, and equipment and utensil storage, and in toilet rooms.
